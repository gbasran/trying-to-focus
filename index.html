FILE_01 :: THE_DRIFT

So the brain in the sim keeps drifting away even though you can clearly see it. That's Executive Dysfunction in a nutshell.

// OBSERVATION

People with ADHD can see the target just fine. The problem is your brain won't actually lock onto it. It's not about willpower or effort, it's about the regulation system literally not working right.

When you leave the brain to click those red boxes, your efficiency tanks. That's Switching Costs. Normal brains switch between tasks pretty easily. ADHD brains? It takes way more energy to refocus after switching.

FILE_02 :: SYSTEM_MYTHS

>> QUERY: HYPERACTIVITY

MYTH: "ADHD means you're bouncing off the walls."

>> STATUS: FALSE

REALITY: It's a regulation disorder. A lot of people have the "Inattentive" type meaning internal chaos, time blindness, and brain fog instead of physical hyperactivity.

>> QUERY: PREVALENCE

MYTH: "Everyone is a little ADHD."

>> STATUS: FALSE

REALITY: Everyone gets distracted sometimes. ADHD is when it happens so often and so severely that it actually messes up your life, job, and health. There's a difference.

FILE_03 :: EXTERNAL_RAM

Working memory is basically broken, so you need external systems to compensate:

  • :: BODY_DOUBLING: Having someone else around helps you stay on task. Their presence anchors your focus.
  • :: VISUAL_TIMERS: Abstract time doesn't work. You need to see it, like that stress bar in the sim.
  • :: GAMIFICATION: Turn boring stuff into a game to trick your brain into releasing dopamine so you can actually start the task.
